The Fall of Ironhold

Released December 15, 2023 | From the Chronicles of Osterra

The dwarven stronghold of Ironhold had stood for seven centuries, its great gates never breached by enemy forces. But on the night of the winter solstice, something stirred in the deep places beneath the mountain—something that had slumbered since before the dwarves first delved those halls.

The first sign of trouble came from the lower mines. Foreman Dolgrim's crew failed to report for the shift change. When a search party was sent to investigate, they found only abandoned tools and a strange darkness that swallowed their torchlight.

"It wasn't natural dark," survivor Brunna Ironbeard would later recount to the refugees gathered at Camp Northwatch. "It moved. It breathed. And when it touched you..." She trailed off, her hands trembling around her tankard of ale.

What emerged from the depths defied description. Some called it shadow made flesh. Others whispered of an ancient evil, one of the Forgotten Ones sealed away by the first mages of the Old Faith. Whatever its nature, the entity moved through Ironhold's halls like a plague, consuming all in its path.

King Thrain IV ordered an immediate evacuation. For the first time in seven hundred years, the great gates of Ironhold were sealed—not to keep enemies out, but to keep something in.

Now, some three thousand dwarven refugees make their way north, seeking shelter with the Northern Alliance. They bring with them only what they could carry: family heirlooms, sacred relics, and terrible memories of the night their homeland fell.

The Northern Alliance has pledged to aid the refugees, but questions remain. What awakened in the depths of Ironhold? Can it be contained behind the sealed gates? And most troubling of all—are there other Forgotten Ones, waiting in dark places across Osterra?

Plot Hook

The Ironhold refugees will feature prominently in the upcoming Rites of Spring event. Players may encounter dwarven NPCs seeking aid, artifacts from the fallen stronghold, or clues about the nature of the entity that destroyed their home.
