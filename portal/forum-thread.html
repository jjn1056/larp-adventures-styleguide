Hey everyone,

I wanted to get some clarification on the Shield Bash skill after the v3.2 rules update. The rulebook says:

"Strike your opponent's shield or weapon with your own shield. Target must take 3 steps backward."

My questions:

  1. Can Shield Bash be used against an opponent who doesn't have a shield? The wording says "shield or weapon" but I've heard different interpretations.
  2. With the new 15-second cooldown, does the timer start when I use the skill, or when the effect is applied?
  3. If the target can't take 3 steps back (wall behind them), what happens?

Thanks for any clarification!

Official Response Posted 4 days ago | #3

Thanks for the questions, Sir Aldric! Here are the official rulings:

1. Target requirements: Shield Bash requires you to hit either a shield OR a weapon. It cannot be used against an unarmed opponent or by hitting their body directly. If they're holding any weapon (including a staff or dagger), that's a valid target.

2. Cooldown timing: The 15-second cooldown starts the moment you make the call "Shield Bash - Knockback!" regardless of whether it hits or is successful.

3. Blocked movement: If the target cannot take the full 3 steps back due to terrain (wall, cliff edge, etc.), they take as many steps as possible and then suffer a "Stagger" effect - they must freeze for 2 seconds as if dazed. This represents the impact of being driven into an obstacle.

I'll add this clarification to the FAQ section of the rulebook. Thanks for bringing it up!
