The Bardic Profession
The Bardic Profession
The Bardic Profession may be taken by any adventurer regardless of Skill Path. Bards are chroniclers, performers, vessels of the magic that flows naturally through the Ley Lines. Channels of that raw energy, heightening it and manipulating it, shaping it to their will with sound, rhythm, and tale.
Bard Non-combat Designation When Fielding With Instruments

Bards with instruments on the field are granted a non-combat designation for LARP Adventures play. Bardic instruments follow similar rules as Bows while in play, and may never be used to attack or block. Since Bards do not pose a direct combat threat on the battlefield while engaged in Bardic activities, attacks of any kind against the Bard while they are playing a physical instrument are considered illegal game actions. We want to encourage Bards to bring their art to the field and to this end we want to promote a safer space for their instruments and the people around those instruments.

Bards holding visible instruments on their person are encouraged to not enter too close to combat, and they are also not allowed to attack anyone by any means, or attempt to use the instrument to block/guard against attacks while they carry their instrument. If a situation arises where the instrument carrying Bard is within close proximity of a melee adversary and engagement would be a likelihood, the Bard is expected to do one of three things identical to options available to a bow user:

- drop their instrument if safe to do so (or safely stash it if the size allows for it [consider a small flute for example, or case for a stringed instrument])
- Attempt to flee, accepting that if caught up to they are open to being attacked from behind
- Surrender, and consider themselves defeated.

It is up to the Bard to enact these options. Failing to do so after being afforded the opportunity, should the Bard be attacked they must take those hits as normal, and consider their spell interrupted.

The intention of the non-combatant denotation and spirit by which this rule is followed is framed upon safety, not to provide an advantage to a Bard/others. Use of the non-combat denotation (or allowing others to exploit this (i.e.) “human shielding” behind the non-targetable person goes against the spirit of these rules and can lead to a Bard having their ability to field with an instrument suspended or removed.

Circumstantial, For purposes of clarity:
If a Bard had an instrument, let’s say a fife, and then stowed it in their pocket, pouch, or bag posing no safety risk, the Bard is then free to engage in combat, and be engaged with in return. The item is considered removed from being an active risk. However, to swap between holding, and putting away such an item, with the intention to abuse the rules, to hide from combat would be seen as a breach of the spirit of the rules and should be avoided.

The non-combat designation does not apply to a person simply singing. The rule is in place regarding instruments for safety reasons. If a person performing by singing is doing so on the field they do invite themselves to be in combat by being on the field, but like any fielding person, if they prefer to avoid combat they do have the ability to attempt to discourage use of force against them by attempting to flee, or surrendering at any time. Bards as any other player may designate themselves as non-combatants, in which case they should follow all rules that apply to non-cambatants, declare themselves as such when entering a scene or mod, and avoid handling weapons in play.

Bardic College Enrollment (optional)

Being a Bard is also about having a supportive space for performers. Folks not advanced in other professions and interested in being a Bard are welcome to join “The Bardic College of Osterra”. There is no tier requirement or skill point investment to enroll in the Bardic College. This allows aspiring players interested in pursuing this profession to role play as Bardic Students, attend the Bards Guild meetings in play, and participate online in the Discord section to learn about the Bardic Profession, observe discussions there, and ask questions.

To seek an engaging role play experience, budding Bardic Students enrolled at the “College” are encouraged to put together a “Song Book” of works, ideas, and items they would be interested in one day performing. By choosing to become a member of the Bardic College, players can (upon surpassing the Apprentice Milestone for being a Bard) choose to do a performance of their art for the realm at a campout or day event, and having accomplished this task, and achieving tier 3, the player gains the in game title, and distinction of being a “Bardic College of Osterra Graduate Student”. The title is just for fun and roleplay. It conveys no real in-game benefits.

Bardic Autodidact (alternative to the “College”)

You don’t have to join the Bardic College to be a Bard. An autodidact is a self-taught person who practices the Bardic Profession without formal instruction, or the benefit of a teacher. This learning style can be fostered by following one's curiosity, using their innate talent, and adapting the Bardic Profession to one's individual style. Self styled Bards are still encouraged to join in Guild meetings and online discussion. Just because you are a rebel, or it doesn’t make sense in character for you to join the Bardic College, it does not exclude you from following the Bardic Profession.
The Resonance Token System
Unlike Path abilities, Bardic techniques draw upon Resonance Tokens, manifestations of shared emotion, performance, and creativity. Whether spoken, sung, strummed, or enacted, these expressions ripple through the Ley Lines of Osterra, connecting the hearts of all who listen.
Performances are powered through Resonance Tokens, used as an essential part of the Bard’s Profession. These tokens represent emotional, magical, and social energy, the pulse of performance itself.
Varying amounts of Resonance Tokens are given to the Bard on their performing journey. Resonance points may be shared among Bards performing together. Using a skill or ability costs an amount of Resonance Tokens equal to that of the tier of the skill or ability used.
Resonance Tokens are replenished each event, or daily at multi-day events, and at the discretion of Organizers.

Milestones of Mastery

Bard Professional Experience

A Profession takes time and effort to master.  In addition to slowly learning, some Bard Tiers are gated by specific Milestones.  Regardless of your Main or Subclass Tier, you will not be able to take Skills above a certain Tier unless you have fulfilled Bard-specific special requirements.
- Like Cross-Class Paths, your Tier in the Bard Profession is the highest-skill-point ability you have.
- Like all Professions, you may not take Skills in Bard until you are Tier 3 in your Main Skill Path.
- The Bard must have at least one Bard Skill in a Tier to progress to the next Tier.
- The Bard cannot take skills in Bard of a Tier higher than their Main Path Tier.
- Unlike Main Paths, a Bard may not take more than one Bard Skill per full event-day (regardless of Skill Points).
- The Bard has specific Milestones that gate Skills. All characters may access Tier 1 and Tier 2 (Apprentice).  There are special requirements that gate access to Tier 3 and 4 skills (Journeyman).  Further requirements must be completed to ascend to full Mastery of the Profession (Tier 5 and 6).
- Profession Focus: Though anyone can dabble, a character may only invest deeply in ONE Profession. Progressing past the Apprentice milestone means limiting (or for full Mastery, eliminating) Skills and Benefits in other Professions.

In-Game Progression
If it did not happen in-game, it did not happen.

Professions are meant to make in-person events more immersive– trading your effort at elevating the Game for unique opportunities and boons.  Though Performance is an art that may require actions outside of game (writing songs, and stories, practicing your chosen instrument, investing your creativity), all Bardic activities resulting in progression require an in-game Performance to ascend Milestones and progress as a Bard in play.

Skills of the Bard:
Milestone I – Apprentice Bard
- You may hold up to 5 Resonance Tokens

- Joining a Band or jam session: You can assist other Bards in a Performance by joining in a Collaborative Song, restoring 1 Resonance token for the lead through accompaniment, and adding your Tier to theirs to elevate the Performance with your collaborative efforts.

All characters who are not Masters of another profession, and are at least Tier 3 in their main path, are eligible to be Apprentices. Apprentices may take Tier 1 and Tier 2 skills.

Tier 1:

Bardic Casting: Bardic casting- in lieu of reading from a book or scroll, a Bard can perform any non-combat spell that they have already obtained via a skill path by performing for 1 minute. They do not need to hold the spellbook; they do have to incorporate any special physreps; physreps denoting the location of the effect, such as Ward ribbons, are still required. This does not apply for any combat spell. This functions for spells of tier up to their Bard Tier; at Bard T6 this functions for any spell. No usage limit.
Requirements: performance of at least 1 minute, expend use of the affected skill.
Phys rep: As defined by the base skill.

Bardic Anecdote: Outside of combat, a warrior or rogue may relay a tale or sing a song in lieu of the physical/rp component of repair or lockpicking skills that they have already obtained via a skill path. This does NOT apply to pickpocket. This functions for skills of up to their Bard Tier; at Bard T6 this functions for any eligible skill. Usage limit as per skill or ability engaged.
Requirements: performance of at least 1 minute, expend use of the affected skill.

Alarm: With a sudden clamor, loud music, or general uproarious boisterousness, the Bard awakens those in the vicinity. Breaks sleep effects (and could possibly break lower tier Silence, trance/stun effects, certain mod effects? At Organizer discretion). Can be used once per day per Bard Milestone.
Requirements: audible performance of at least 30s.

Tier 2

Peace Offering: Outside combat, with a performance of at least 1 minute, the Bard may improve the base attitude of an NPC by 1 step. The improved attitude can then be altered by PC actions as normal. If the NPC is attacked, their attitude reverts to base. Can be used once per day.
Requirements: a performance of at least 1 minute.

Legend Lore: Through your experience with either the Bardic College or the traditions of your own people, you are knowledgeable about the great tales and songs that recorded the histories of old. This ability allows you to potentially gain greater insight regarding an artifact, location, or being of some renown.
Requirements: Organizer contribution, 3 minute performance.

Sleep: When not in combat, you can weave a song of subtle dreaming around your foes. You can target up to one player, or NPC per Bard Tier (increasing to 3 at Bard Milestone 3), and lull them into trance or sleep. This effect can be broken by an rp effort to wake the target (don't touch people without their consent please), by any kind of damage, by any ability that removes status effects, or by Alarm. It may also be broken by environmental effects. Certain NPCs may be immune to it, at mod runner or Organizer discretion. If used on a character under the effect of Iron Will or Mind Shield, the use of this skill is expended with no effect. This skill may be used once per day per Bard Milestone achieved.
Requirements: Alarm, a performance of at least 1 minute

Milestone II – Journeyman Bard
To advance to Journeyman, an Apprentice must…
  - Possess an instrument or LARP-appropriate songbook
  - Have at least 3 Bard skills, with at least one skill in each previous Tier
  - Promote and give a 5 minute performance at a Campout or Festival, which cannot be the same event at which you took your first Bard skill
Being a performer, you understand that the higher levels of your art can only be achieved through focus and dedication. By taking a Tier 3-4 skill in Bard, you may only take Tier 1 and Tier 2 skills in other Professions.  All Tier 3+ skills from other Professions are lost

Journeyman also introduces an ability central to the Bard called “Bardic Inspiration”.
Further, a Bard may now:
  - Increase their Resonance Token pool to 7 total.

  - Perform one Collaborative Song combining two Bards’ effects.

  - “Echo” an effect by spending 1 Resonance Token immediately after another Bard’s performance, allowing for the same effect to occur an additional time.
  - A player that has attended the Bardic College attains the title of Bardic College of Osterra Graduate
Explanation of the Bardic Inspiration mechanic: While within arms reach of the Bard, the Bard performs audibly for 1 minute, the Bard may convey a gold colored belt favor with a black icon on it that conveys a special boon drawn from the inspiration of a storied champion of old. The conclusion of this performance must contain the spoken phrase “I (player name) invoke Bardic Inspiration”.

The Bard can convey multiple favors via this method during the same time up to the maximum amount they are allowed to possess, one per Bard Tier (3 at Tier 3, 4 at Tier 4 , and so forth).
No single person can wear more than one Bardic favor at a time and the Bard can only convey favors to others, not themselves. (Though Bards could inspire other Bards and obtain favor via this fashion).

    - The Bard favor is represented by a gold belt flag that must be visible and hang at least 6 inches long by 2 inches across.
    - Favors obtained expire upon death of the player wearing them, but can be recharged by the Bard performing again.

Tier 3

Bardic Inspiration of The Elemental Mage of The Sea (Surf):
Denoted by a gold colored belt favor with a black diamond icon. This favor, while worn and active, bestows the wearer with the ability to master the element of water and walk upon it unhindered like a great Mage in command of the sea from the ancient stories. The effect of this ability can be interpreted at discretion of the Organizer- I.E. sure you can walk across water, but liquid surfaces like acid or lava may play out differently at Organizer discretion.
All characters affected must interact with the scene.
Requirements: performance of at least 60 seconds
Phys rep: gold belt favor with black diamond icon

Bringer of Word-Fame: Once per season, the Bard may create and publicly perform an original work glorifying the actions of another character, this piece should be at least 3 minutes in length (or per Organizer approval). The subject character gains a skill point. This work may also qualify for Public Entertainer.  A Bard may not use this skill to benefit any single character more than once.
Requirements: performance of at least three minutes.
Phys rep: A signed copy of a created piece, such as lyrics, or choreography notes, to be given to the recipient.

Harmonize With Creature: The magic of music or performance can tame the wild beast. Through shared song, you may enthrall, and come to understand a creature on a deeper level than language. The Bard coaxes a creature who cannot use language into participating in a shared performance (humming along to a tune, swaying with a drumbeat, mimicking a dance) for at least 90 seconds and so gains the ability to communicate with them. The effect lasts for up to 10 minutes. May be used once per day, or twice per day at Bard Tier 5.
Requirements: performance of at least 90 seconds, RP.

Tier 4

Bardic Inspiration of The Rogue:
Denoted by a gold colored favor with a black arrow icon. The favor while worn, and active, bestows upon the wearer the nimbleness of the legendary rogues in the stories of old. It confers the ability “Freedom of Movement”. This makes the wearer immune from abilities that would make a character frozen or paralyzed. It also allows the wearer to stand in the presence of larger foes like giants (which would otherwise force players onto their knees).
Requirements: performance of at least 60 seconds
Phys rep: gold belt favor with a black arrow icon

Public Entertainer: Once per season, a Bard may promote and give a concert of at least 4 minutes (per Organizer approval). Upon completion of the performance, the Bard gains a skill point. This work may also qualify for Bringer of Word-Fame. You can only benefit from one skill point gain from professions per event.
Requirements: performance of at least 4 minutes, and publicly advertise, or herald the performance.

Student of the Ages: Your familiarity with, and studies into the depth and breadth of the Bardic arts gives you insight into the cultural background of Osterra. This ability allows you to potentially glean some of the context of an object or event - usually a phrase, or a few sentences at most. Can use 1/day, and may augment Legend Lore.
Requirements: Organizer contribution, performance of 2 minutes.
Milestone III – Master Bard
To progress to Master, a Journeyman must…

    - Have taken 6 Bard Skills, including one from each Tier. Each instance of a Skill that can be retaken counts towards this total.
    - Complete a 5 minute performance in which the Bard demonstrates their skill during a festival or campout.
    - Author, score, or otherwise produce a performance that is an account of some major storyline of the last year, or major lore topic.
    - A Graduate may now claim their own musical title at the Bardic College of Osterra. It may be Teacher, Maestro/Maestra, Band Leader, Master of Music, Doctor of Musical Arts, Composer, Musicologist, use your creativity and have fun with it you’ve earned Mastery of The Bardic Profession
Mastery demands a performer’s time and attention utterly, not only to pursue greatness, but to nurture the talents of other Bards. Once you achieve Mastery in Bard, you lose access to all skills from other Professions.
Furthermore, a Bard may now:
    - Increase their Resonance Tokens to 10 total.

    - Perform a Grand Performance, combining any number of effects and drawing power from nearby allies’ emotion or song.

    - Craft or attune to Harmonic Relics—unique instruments that store or shape Resonance.
Tier 5

Bardic Inspiration of The Healer:
Denoted by the gold belt favor with black chalice icon. The favor, while worn, and active bestows upon the bearer blessings of the healers of legend. The wearer when targeted by any healing spell or potion of any tier/strength level will find themselves fully restored, even from beyond death.
Requirements: performance of at least 60 seconds
Phys rep: gold belt favor with black chalice icon

Bardic Circle: Inspired by the Bardic circles from our great community. A Bard knows that grand stories, inspiring dances, and brilliant performances can only be delivered to minds and hearts in a place of safety.  Outside of Combat fields, the Bard may create a specially warded area that can reasonably be considered a venue-- operating as a zone that allows entry for all and disallows combat within it. This zone is sustained by performance within its boundary-- singing, storytelling, dancing, or other Bardic activities-- no matter who the performer is. After 5 minutes without performance, Bardic Venue dissipates.
Limitations: No character who has engaged in combat within 2 minutes may enter the Bardic Venue-- it is not to be used as a place to escape, hide, or create an advantage in a fight. The Bardic Venue must not be in an area denoted as a combat field. Can be used once per day. Requirements: Two and a half minute performance to begin the Bardic Circle.
Phys rep: Lilac ribbon (may not need to be continuous, as needed to mark Ward boundary),

Tier 6

Bardic Inspiration of The Warrior::
Denoted by a gold colored belt favor with a black sword icon. The favor, while worn, and active bestows upon the bearer the spirit of a great hero of legend that knew how to vanquish their foe. The favor allows the wielder to strike with "True Strike" on a named creature, monster, or NPC, meaning the damage type the foe would be most vulnerable against. i.e. If fighting a troll, your sword would swing fire damage. If fighting another Osterran or being that has no true weaknesses though for example, this boon would have strikes cause double the normal damage, this can be left up to discretion of the Organizer/mod runner overseeing that combat, and may not always apply.-i.e. Some rarer creatures, monsters, or unique beings (Ferdinand being an example) exist that require special artifacts to inflict damage. This should be used primarily for PVE, and should not be used in PVP without player consent.
Requirements: performance of at least 60 seconds
Phys rep: gold belt favor with black sword icon

Taunting Jest: At the start of combat, the player loudly declares that “I (player name) am invoking Taunting Jest against (named target)”. This invocation is followed up by making a jest, insult, or boast aimed at the named target — audible to all. This will taunt the target into prioritizing you, the performing Bard as their target. This effect lasts until:
      - You are reduced to 0 hit points
      - You disappear from the fighting area.
      - Your target is interrupted by another person having targeted them.
Cannot be used on the same character twice in a season. Can be used once per day. Requirements: RP

Harmonic Relics
Within the Bardic College, certain relics have become synonymous with mastery and legend. These tools channel the world’s harmony through craftsmanship and art, blending form and function in equal measure.
These Harmonic Relics should follow the example structures below:
Instrument
	Property
	Quirk
	Arion’s Harp of Quench Creek
	May store 1 Focus token per day. The stored Focus may be released as part of any healing, restoration, or Song of Renewal performance.
	Must be played with bare hands near running water.
	Vidarian Resonator Drum
	Performances near the field of combat Cost half the amount of Resonance Tokens, rounded up.
	Deafening indoors; anyone within 10 ft must pause a beat before speaking or casting.
	Flute of Echoes
	Once per day, may repeat the last short performance or spell-like song the Bard performed, duplicating its effect at half strength (range or duration halved). Activated by playing the first few notes of the previous performance.
	If not emptied, it replays its stored sound at sunset
	Other instruments may be created through cooperation between Master Bards and Artificers, whose combined craft may forge new “Harmonic Relics” of unique power provided they are also Organizer approved. A Bard may only have one Harmonic Relic in play at a time.
