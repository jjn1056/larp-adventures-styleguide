The Mage's Path
The Mage’s Path

Skills, spells, and abilities for Mages
LARP Adventures
Tier 0 Abilities
Basic Weapon Proficiency: The Mage learns the basic combat and safety rules and the use of hand weapons and throwables. For Mages, this includes one handed weapons, staves, LARP safe throwables, and two handed bladed weapons.

Read/Write Arcana: The Mage can decipher and read magic runes. This ability is represented by a rune cipher in their spellbook. The Mage is able to cast spells while walking or standing still.

Basic Armor Proficiency: The Mage is allowed 1 floating Armor Point (AP) when they physically wear a hat, head covering, or other protective LARP styled headgear. The Mage inherently has no Armor Proficiency and cannot receive benefits to their AP total from physically wearing armor. At Tier 4, the Mage may treat this skill as the prerequisite to Cross-Train the skill Armor Training (Warrior Tier 2 skill) to increase their AP to 1 and may take the Warrior skill Armor Training at later Tiers to allow a maximum possibility of 2 AP as a main-class Mage. A Mage untrained in Armor is unable to cast spells while wearing Armor.

Light/Darkness: The Mage can create magical light or magical darkness.
Limitations: Can only have one in play at a time
Wordcount: 10 words
Physical representation: Arcane focus, a light producing implement or black cloth
Uses: Unlimited
Area: 10’ radius/63’ circumference circle
Duration: Until dismissed
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Sense Arcana: The Mage can sense if an object has arcane properties, but not any details of powers or effects.
Limitations: Use on a single person or object
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 10 words
Uses: 3x per day +1 per Mage Tier
Area: n/a
Duration: Until dismissed
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None
Tier 1 Abilities
Repair Minor: The Mage repairs a single, small unmagical object 12” or smaller or one hit location of armor.
Limitations: Single person or object
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 25 words
Uses: 3x per day +1 per Mage Tier above Tier 1
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Divine Arcana: Once an object is defined as magical via Sense Arcana, the Mage can sense its arcane properties and any details of the object’s powers or effects.
Limitations: Single person or object
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 25 words
Uses: 1 per Mage Tier per day
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: Sense Arcana

Arcane Armor I: The Mage protects themselves and others. This spell, cast on one person at a time, absorbs the first point of damage taken by the target, regardless of that hit’s location.
Limitations: Once 1 point of damage is absorbed, the spell fades and the belt flag should be removed at the first opportunity.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. A red belt flag marked with one black stripe.
Wordcount: 25 words
Uses: 2x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 1
Area: n/a
Duration: Until 1 point of damage is absorbed by the target.
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Ward Location: The Mage may now create a protected area with a ward. Warded areas cannot be entered or exited until the ward is lifted or broken, except for those the spellcaster specifically allows in and/or out. Warded objects may not be opened or destroyed without breaking the ward first. Strength of Tier increase possible with Arcane Confluence.
Limitations: Projectiles may not be fired into or out of a warded location. A ward may not be fought through.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. Yellow ribbon around location or object, and a written message denoting tier, allowed entrants, and caster's name.
Wordcount: 25 words
Uses: 1x per Mage Tier per day. A Mage may have one warded location active per Mage Tier they possess.
Area: 20’ radius/126’ circumference circle
Duration: Until dismissed
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Mage’s Staff: The Mage gains the ability to use a staff imbued with the Mage’s energy. The staff may act as a Focus for spellcasting, and may be considered an extension of the Mage where touch is concerned for casting and game play.
Limitations: Regardless of whether this staff is wielded in one hand or two, it always does 1 point of damage. Once a staff is imbued with Magic in this way, it can not be further enchanted by standard means.
Physical representation: LARP safe staff
Wordcount: n/a
Uses: n/a
Area: n/a
Duration: Permanent
Damage: Magic 1
Prerequisite: None

Arcane Confluence: The best Mages learn that they can get further by working together. Arcane Confluence can be used to upcast specifically noted spells with the aid of other Mages. The Mage who casts the spell receives +1 to their effective Tier for each Mage trained in Arcane Confluence working together. Any spell that receives additional uses per tier or can be upcast to be of a higher tier is compatible with this skill. A Mage may help add to or break a ward so long as they possess this skill, even if they have not yet taken the spell being enhanced with Arcane Confluence.
Limitations: The invocation must be initiated by a Mage who already possesses the training for the spell in question.
Wordcount: Spells cast with Arcane Confluence take double the amount of words they normally would (a reread of the spell or certain parts of the spell is appropriate).
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Uses: n/a
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Magic Seal: A Mage’s secrets must be guarded. A Mage can thread some of their magic into the sealing of a missive, letter, tome, or other written material, including Profession documents, to prevent any other than the intended recipient from opening it. A Magic Seal can only be broken by the unintended recipient via Break Arcana. Strength of Tier increase possible with Arcane Confluence
Limitations: n/a
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. Silver and Green ribbon wrapped around the written material. A wax stamp or drawn symbol, the name of the intended recipient, and the Tier of the casting Mage marked on the exterior of the written material.
Wordcount: 25 words
Uses: 2x per day +1 per Mage Tier above Tier 1
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None
Tier 2 Abilities:
Scrolls & Potions: The Mage gains the ability to produce scrolls and potions of the spells they have already trained in. Potions may be consumed by anyone. Scrolls may only be utilized by those who have taken Read/Write Arcana.
For more detailed rulings on Potions or Scrolls, please refer to the full Scroll/Potion ruleset for limitations and mechanics.

Comprehend Language: The Player can understand any one chosen language, written or spoken. This can be cast on the Mage themselves or another Player.
Limitations: n/a
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 35 words
Uses: 3x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 2
Area: n/a
Duration: 10 minutes
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Elemental Protection: The Mage is able to weave a spell to protect a player, object, or area from the effects of a chosen element (Fire, Ice, Lightning, Necrotic). This protection negates the first instance of damage or a single effect from the chosen element.
Limitations: The caplet or ribbon should be removed after the damage is absorbed.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. A red caplet placed on the player, object, or area under protection.
Wordcount: 35 words
Uses: 1x per Tier per day + 1 for every Mage Tier above 2
Area: 1 player, 1 object, or 1 5’ radius/31’ circumference circle
Duration: 4 hours or until the enchanted player is Mortally Wounded
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Push: A Mage may use their authority over the Leylines and their spellcasting abilities to Push another player back. The spell must include the number of steps the opposing player is pushed back.
Limitations: Cannot be used on the same player twice in one day.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. Two outstretched arms in a pushing motion.
Wordcount: 10 words
Uses: 1x per Mage Tier per day
Area: The opposing player is pushed back 3 steps per Mage Tier.
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Break Arcana: Once an object’s magical aspects are defined via Divine Arcana, the Mage can dispel the magic on a single object, player, enchantment, or warded location. Can be cast at a higher Tier than Tier 2. Strength of Tier increase possible with Arcane Confluence.
Limitations: The Arcana must first be examined through the use of Divine Arcana. The spell is only successful if the Mage Tier of the caster is equal to or higher than the Tier of the magic being dispelled.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 35 words. Can be upcast with an additional 15 words per Mage Tier above Tier 2
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per Mage Tier.
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: Divine Arcana

Magic Shield: The Mage conjures a shield of magical energy for themselves. The Magic Shield can be used to block Combat Magic Spells an amount of times equal to the caster of the shield’s Mage’s Tier.
Limitations: The Mage may only have 1 Magic Shield in play at any given time. The Mage’s Magic Shield can only be wielded by its caster.
Physical representation: Green shield 27” or less in diameter.
Wordcount: 35 words
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 2
Area: n/a
Duration: 4 hours
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: Shield Control (Warrior or Healer Tree Tier 0 skill)

Advanced Magic Seal: A Mage’s secrets must be closely guarded. The text of a magically sealed document is unreadable to those not bound to its delivery. Any who attempt to read the document must sign their true and full name on the page. Advanced Magic Seal can be broken with Break Arcana. Strength of Tier increase possible with Arcane Confluence.
Limitations: n/a
Physical representation: Silver and Green ribbon wrapped around the written material. A wax stamp or drawn symbol, the name of the intended recipient, and the Tier of the casting Mage marked on the exterior of the written material. A designation reading “Invisible Ink” on the interior of the page.
Wordcount: 35 words
Uses: 1x per day +1 per Mage Tier above Tier 3
Area: n/a
Duration: Unlimited
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: Magic Seal

Cross-Training:  At Tier 2 you may train in one of the other Skill Paths. Cross Training skills are repeatable. This skill allows the player to explore Tier 0 and Tier 1 skills in other path trees at the cost of 2 skill points and require all necessary prerequisites of the specific skill.
Warrior Training 1: The Mage dedicates time to learning the craft of warriors. They can choose one tier 1 or lower ability from the Warriors Skill path.
Healer Training 1: The Mage dedicates time to learning the craft of healers. They can choose one tier 1 or lower ability from the Healer skill path.
Rogue Training 1: The Mage studies and watches the rogues around them. They can choose one tier 1 or lower ability from the Rogue skill path.

Steady Spellcaster allows the Mage to move with complete freedom around the battlefield while still maintaining their focus to cast a spell. The Mage only needs to cast this on themselves once.
Limitations: All other standard limitations to casting remain.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 50 words
Uses: n/a
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: Arcane Armor I

Repair Major: The Mage repairs a single, medium unmagical object 48” or smaller or all hit locations of armor for a single player.
Limitations: Single person or object
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 50 words
Uses: 3x per day +1 per Mage Tier above Tier 3
Area: n/a

Limitations: Once 2 points of damage are absorbed, the spell fades and the belt flag should be removed at the first opportunity.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. A red belt flag marked with two black stripes.
Wordcount: 50 words

Speak Language: The Player can read, write, and converse in any one chosen language. This can be cast on the Mage themselves or another Player. Comprehend Language is not required to cast in advance of Speak Language.
Limitations: n/a
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 50 words
Uses: 3x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 3
Area: n/a
Duration: 10 minutes
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: Comprehend Language

Rune Bound Mage’s Staff: As the Mage’s skill with magic grows, so does the deadliness of the magic they’ve imbued into their staff. The staff may act as an Arcane Focus for spellcasting, and may be considered an extension of the Mage where touch is concerned for casting and game play. The Mage may gain additional mana points up to half the player’s Mage tier rounded up for use with the ‘Scrolls and Potions’ skill for the creation of Scrolls only.
Limitations: Once a staff is imbued with Magic in this way, it can not be further enchanted by standard means.
Physical representation: LARP safe staff
Wordcount: n/a
Uses: n/a
Area: n/a
Duration: Permanent
Damage: Magic 2 when held with 2 hands, Magic 1 when held with 1 hand.
Prerequisite: Mage’s Staff

Magic Missile: The Mage summons three bolts of magical energy that can be thrown at their enemies. Magic Missile can also be used to destroy objects such as chests, gates, walls, siege engines and more. May be blocked by shields. Normal armor rules apply.
Limitations: n/a
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. 3” minimum black spellball or alternatively character themed coreless black throwing weapons such as darts, knives, or axes.
Wordcount: 15 words summons the first missile. 10 more words summons the second missile. 10 more words summons the third missile.
Uses: 3x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 3
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: 1 Magic damage per bolt
Prerequisite: None

Speak with Dead: The Mage can communicate with the dead. The soul of the deceased must answer these questions truthfully and to the best of their abilities. The soul is unaware of any questions asked or answers given when returned to life. The soul is not required to answer additional questions with any subsequent uses of the spell.
Limitations: The Mage is able to ask 3 questions.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 50 words
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 3
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None
Tier 4 Abilities:
Spell Storing: The Mage can now store spells they’ve already learned into their chosen Focus for later use.
Limitations: n/a
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 5 words that include the invocation line of the chosen spell
Uses: At the start of each day, the Mage may store an amount of spells equal to half their Mage Tier rounded up. The same spell may be chosen multiple times.
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Covenant: The Mage oversees a magically binding agreement between two entities. The Covenant does not break until the deal is fulfilled on both sides. The document should include the terms of the agreement and the consequences if the Covenant is broken. The Covenant cannot be broken by Break Arcana.
Limitations: n/a
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. A written parchment signed by the Mage and any additional parties.
Wordcount: 75 words
Uses: The Mage may have only one active Covenant at any given time.
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: Advanced Magic Seal

Mind Shield: The Player’s mind is shielded from mental interference. They will not succumb to mental magics, psionic mind control, or other psionic effects including Compel Truth
Limitations: Mind Shield does not negate Fear.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 75 words
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 4
Area: n/a
Duration: 1 hour or player death
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Anti-Magic Aura: The Mage is able to weave a spell to protect a player, object, or area from the effects of magic. This protection negates the first instance of magical damage or magical effect.
Limitations: The caplet or ribbon should be removed after the damage is absorbed.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. A silver caplet placed on the player, a silver ribbon placed on an object, or a silver ribbon around the area under protection.
Wordcount: 75 words
Uses: 1x per Tier per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 4
Area: 1 player, 1 object, or 1 5’ radius/31’ circumference circle
Duration: 4 hours or until the enchanted player is Mortally Wounded
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Mental Duel: The Mage draws another spellcaster into an immediate mental contest. The other spellcaster must enter the duel with the Mage. The winner of the duel chooses a combat spell to remove from the spellbook of the loser; the removal lasts for one game day. The duelers are untouchable by others while engaged in the duel. Examples of Mental Duels may include but are not limited to Rock-Paper-Scissors, a Dance Battle, a Staring Contest.
Limitations: If the challenged player is not, in fact, a spellcaster, the spell fizzles and is spent with no effect.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 75 words
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 4
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Lightning Bolt: The Mage summons a great electrical charge of magical energy in the form of a single Lightning Bolt. Lightning Bolts can also be used to destroy objects such as chests, gates, walls, siege engines and more. Bypasses metal armor. May be blocked by shields, but the shield is then destroyed.
Limitations: n/a
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. 3” minimum blue spellball or alternatively character themed coreless blue throwing weapons such as bolts, darts, knives, or axes.
Wordcount: 40 words
Uses: 2x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 4
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: 2 Magic Lightning damage
Prerequisite: None

Animate Dead: The Mage’s mastery of death has advanced to the point where they may reanimate a lifeless body to create a minion to serve them. The Mage may select to animate a dead creature as a Zombie (2HP, movement limited to walking) or a Skeleton (1HP, moves freely, ignores thrusts and arrows). Both attack with Necrotic Damage. Both take double damage from Holy damage. Both are dependent on orders from their master and can only follow the most basic of commands. In the absence of commands from their master, they attack the nearest creature until destroyed. When players regain life, they do not regain any memories of their actions while animated.
Limitations: Players may decline to be animated.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. Skeleton mask or gray headband, Zombie mask or black headband
Wordcount: 75 words
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per every 2 Mage/Healer Tiers. A Mage may only have 1 Animated Dead in play per 2 Mage/Healer Tiers.
Area: n/a
Duration: 10 minutes or until slain. If not slain within 10 minutes, the undead player may choose to remain an undead minion to the casting Mage for only as long as they wish.
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: Speak with Dead

Cross-Training: At Tier 4 you may continue to train in one of the other Skill Paths. Cross Training skills are repeatable. This skill allows the player to explore Tier 2 skills in other path trees at the cost of 4 skill points and require all necessary prerequisites of the specific skill.
Warrior Training 2: The Mage dedicates time to learning the craft of warriors. They can choose one tier 2 or lower ability from the Warriors Skill path.
Healer Training 2: The Mage dedicates time to learning the craft of healers. They can choose one tier 2 or lower ability from the Healer skill path.
Rogue Training 2: The Mage studies and watches the rogues around them. They can choose one tier 2 or lower ability from the Rogue skill path.
Tier 5 Abilities:
Mass Repair: The Mage is able to immediately repair large objects and large numbers of objects all at once. A single large item or a number of small items equal to the Mage’s Tier en masse are able to be repaired. As the Mage gains Tiers, they are able to repair even larger structures. For the purposes of this spell, armor, weapons, shields and other hand held items count as small items and are repaired to their full structure or armor point value. Anything not easily lifted by a single person or that is obviously large, such as a castle gate or the hull of a ship, counts as large. Strength of Tier increase possible with Arcane Confluence.
Limitations: This spell does not restore broken magical properties.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 100 words. The words of the spell must include the terminology of what the Mage intends to repair.
Uses: 1x per day +1 per Mage Tier above Tier 5. May only be used once per encounter.
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: Repair Major

Enchant Weapon/Item: The Mage imbues a single weapon or item with magic.
Limitations: This spell does not stack with weapons or items that are already enchanted with Magic or Holy capabilities.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. Green ribbon tied to the weapon or item.
Wordcount: 100 words
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above 5
Area: n/a
Duration: 4 hours or until the wielder of the weapon or item is mortally wounded
Damage: +1 Magic Damage. All damage from the weapon is now considered magical.
Prerequisite: Repair Major

Fireball: The Mage gathers a sphere of magical energy in the form of a single Fireball. Fireball can also be used to destroy objects such as chests, gates, walls, siege engines and more. Bypasses both shields and non-metal armor.
Limitations: n/a
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. 5” minimum red spellball or alternatively character themed coreless red throwing weapons such as darts, knives, or axes.
Wordcount: 70 words
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 5
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: 4 Magic Fire damage
Prerequisite: None

Create Undead Knight: The Mage’s continued study in the necromantic arts allows them to increase the strength of their undead minions. The Mage may now create their Skeleton or Zombie as a Knight, which triples their undead HP and allows them to speak and follow more advanced commands. The Knight may also use Mighty Blow (Warrior Skill, Tier 4) a single time if they wield a two handed weapon. When players regain life, they do not regain any memories of their actions while animated.
Limitations: Players may decline to be animated.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. Even meaner Skeleton mask or gray headband with 2 skulls, even meaner Zombie mask or black headband with 2 skulls
Wordcount: 100 words
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per every 2 Mage/Healer Tiers. A Mage may only have 1 Undead Knight in play per 3 Mage/Healer Tiers.
Area: n/a
Duration: 10 minutes or until slain. If not slain within 10 minutes, the undead player may choose to remain an undead minion to the casting Mage for only as long as they wish.
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: Animate Dead
Tier 6 Abilities:
Summon Creature: The Mage conjures an NPC creature to do their bidding. The NPC summoned will be based on the adventure setting and at the discretion of Game Officials. Alternatively, a Unit or Faction may create a creature concept and have that monster be played by a teammate with Game Officials approval.
Limitations: Requires coordination with the Game Officials. Cannot be used in conjunction with Spell Storing.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 100 words
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 6
Area: n/a
Duration: 10 minutes per Mage Tier. Unless the Mage is able to cast Summon Creature again, once the duration of the spell is completed the creature will revert to its natural state and either attack the nearest players or flee.
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Ensorcelled Armament: The Mage is able to use any weapon of their choice as their Arcane Focus. The Mage may now take damage from a single, mundane stroke without interrupting their casting. The Mage may now cast without referencing their spellbook; spells subsequently must be memorized. The Mage may use their Ensorcelled Armament to cast Combat Magic via touch if Spell Storing has been used with this weapon as the focus.
Limitations: This spell can only be cast once on a single weapon.
Physical representation: LARP safe weapon
Wordcount: 150 words that must include a description of the Mage’s chosen weapon.
Uses: n/a
Area: n/a
Duration: Permanent
Damage: the LARP weapon chosen now does Magic damage and cannot be further enchanted.
Prerequisite: Spell Storing

Modify Memory: The Mage can alter several minutes of a player’s in-character memory. Strength of Tier increase possible with Arcane Confluence.
Limitations: The Mage can alter X minutes equal to the Mage’s Tier. Cannot be used to change memory that occurred on a previous day.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 140 words that include how the memory is being modified
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 6
Area: n/a
Duration: Permanent
Damage: n/a
Prerequisite: None

Ice Storm: The Mage drains the heat from all around them to create a lashing, frozen storm. All types of mundane armor and shields are ineffective against Ice Storm.
Limitations: If used with Spell Storing, the first 50 words can be stored but the remaining 90 must be spoken in order to convey the spell’s effects.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. 3” minimum white spellball or alternatively character themed coreless white throwing weapons such as icicles, darts, knives, or axes. The Mage may throw 6 spellballs at Tier 6 and 2 additional spellballs per every additional Tier above 6.
Wordcount: 140 words. Effects begin after 50 words and the Mage can move freely around the field while continuing to cast the spell, stopping briefly to state effects as the spell is in play.
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per Mage Tier above Tier 6
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: First strike brings a foe to a slow walk, Second strike freezes them in place for ten seconds, Third strike creates a mortalled, frozen body.
Prerequisite: None

Necrotic Blast: The Mage draws dark, necrotic energy to release upon their foes.  Necrotic Blast can also be used to destroy objects such as chests, gates, walls, siege engines and more. Destroys shields on strike.
Limitations: n/a
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. 6” minimum black spellball or alternatively character themed coreless black throwing weapons such as darts, knives, or axes.
Wordcount: 80 words
Uses: 1x per day + 1 per every 2 Mage Tiers
Area: n/a
Duration: n/a
Damage: 4 Magic Necrotic damage to NPCs. Instant death to PCs regardless of armor.

Animate Group of Dead: The Mage approaches the pinnacle of their necromantic arts as they are able to now animate a hoard of undead. The Mage may select to animate a dead creature as a Zombie (2HP, movement limited to walking) or a Skeleton (1HP, moves freely, ignores thrusts and arrows). Both attack with Necrotic Damage. Both take double damage from Holy damage. Both are dependent on orders from their master and can only follow the most basic of commands. In the absence of commands from their master, they attack the nearest creature until destroyed. When players regain life, they do not regain any memories of their actions while animated. Strength of Tier increase possible with Arcane Confluence.

Cross-Training: At Tier 6 you may continue to train in one of the other Skill Paths. Cross Training skills are repeatable. This skill allows the player to explore Tier 3 skills in other path trees at the cost of 6 skill points and require all necessary prerequisites of the specific skill.
Warrior Training 3: The Mage dedicates time to learning the craft of warriors. They can choose one tier 3 or lower ability from the Healer skill path.
Healer Training 3: The Mage dedicates time to learning the craft of healers. They can choose one tier 3 or lower ability from the Healer skill path.
Rogue Training 3: The Mage studies and watches the rogues around them. They can choose one tier 3 or lower ability from the Rogue skill path.
Tier 7 Abilities
Banish Creature: The Mage is able to send an NPC creature away. This can be used on regular NPCs and creatures brought into play via the Summon Creature spell.
Limitations: Requires coordination with the Game Officials. The Mage can only banish creatures of a lower tier than the Mage. Strength of Tier increase possible with Arcane Confluence. Cannot be used in conjunction with Spell Storing.

Wordcount: 150 words

Disintegrate: The Mage draws dark energy that coalesces into a swirling ball of chaos, crackling with power. Bypasses all shields and armor. Disintegrate can also be used to destroy objects such as a gate.
Limitations: A disintegrated player can only be brought back via a Greater Power or the Resurrection spell.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus. 6” minimum black spellball or alternatively character themed coreless black throwing weapons such as darts, knives, or axes.

Locate Object: The Mage is able to locate any singular object that they have previously seen or held.
Limitations: Requires coordination with the Game Officials for the spell’s Vision. The Mage is immobile while casting. The Mage is unable to locate an object stored within an extra-dimension space.
Physical representation: Arcane Focus
Wordcount: 200 words performed in a ceremony setting
Uses: 1x per day
Area: n/a

Glossary
Being a Mage at LARP Adventures: A Glossary

Roleplay is expected with the casting of every spell.

Every spell must end with the clearly stated invocation in English “I cast (spell name).” These words can count to the total word count for that spell.

Elder Futhark is the standard runic system used for magic at LARP Adventures

Casting a spell requires great concentration. A Mage may remain stationary or move at a walk while casting until Steady Spellcaster is achieved.

An Arcane Focus is a non-weapon object that flavors the Mage’s spellcasting.

If a Mage is interrupted while casting, they must begin the casting anew. The failed spell does count against the Mage’s use-per-day.

Magical Enchantments and Holy Enchantments may not stack.

A Mage must have their spellbook on their person and reference it while casting a spell (until Spell Storing and/or Ensorcelled Armament are achieved).

It is a Mage’s responsibility to communicate with the Game Runner what spells they have prepared that may impact gameplay beyond standard means.

All spells are considered to have a range of touch or thrown, as appropriate.

Combat Spells are spells that are used to attack in combat: Magic Missile, Lightning Bolt, Fireball, Ice Storm, Necrotic Blast, Disentigrate

Mage’s Staff, Rune Bound Mage’s Staff, and Ensorcelled Armament weapons can only be wielded by the Mage who cast those spells upon said weapons.The weapon, if in another’s hands, is still usable but completely mundane.

Arcane Confluence is compatible with: Ward Location, Magic Seal, Break Arcana, Advanced Magic Seal, Modify Memory, Animate Group Undead, Banish Creature
