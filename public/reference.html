Overview

Combat in LARP Adventures uses foam-padded weapons to simulate melee and ranged attacks. All combat is conducted safely with clear rules for engagement, damage, and effects.

"The goal of combat is for everyone to have fun while telling an exciting story. Safety and sportsmanship always come first."

This document covers:

  • Safety requirements and weapon standards
  • How to deal and receive damage
  • Armor and defensive mechanics
  • Combat skills and special abilities
  • What happens when your character falls in battle

Safety First

Safety is the foundation of all combat at LARP Adventures. These rules are non-negotiable.

The Cardinal Rules
  1. No head shots. Intentional strikes to the head or neck are prohibited.
  2. No thrusting with non-thrust-safe weapons. Only approved thrusting weapons may be used for stabbing motions.
  3. Pull your swings. Strikes should be firm enough to feel, not hard enough to hurt.
  4. Check your weapons. All weapons must pass safety inspection before each event.
  5. Respect holds. When anyone calls "HOLD," all action stops immediately.

The "HOLD" Call

Any player may call "HOLD" at any time if there is a safety concern. When you hear "HOLD":

  1. Stop all movement and combat immediately
  2. Take a knee if you are able
  3. Remain silent and wait for the issue to be resolved
  4. Resume play only when "LAY ON" is called

Weapon Safety Standards

Requirement Standard
Foam padding Minimum 5/8" closed-cell foam on all striking surfaces
Core material Fiberglass, carbon fiber, or approved alternatives (no PVC, wood, or metal)
Tip construction 2" of foam beyond core end for thrusting weapons
Overall condition No exposed core, tears, or excessive wear

Damage & Calls

When you strike an opponent, you must call your damage clearly and audibly. If your opponent cannot hear your call, the effect does not apply.

Basic Damage Calls

State the amount of damage with each swing:

  • "One!" - Deal 1 point of damage
  • "Two!" - Deal 2 points of damage
  • "Three!" - Deal 3 points of damage (etc.)

Damage Modifiers

Call Effect
"[X] Magic!" Damage bypasses non-magical armor
"[X] Silver!" Affects creatures vulnerable to silver
"[X] Fire!" Fire damage; may ignite flammable materials
"[X] Ice!" Cold damage; may slow target
"[X] Poison!" Poison damage; may have additional effects

Receiving Damage

When you are hit:

  1. Acknowledge the hit (a grunt, stagger, or verbal acknowledgment)
  2. Subtract the damage from your armor first, then hit points
  3. When you reach 0 hit points, you fall unconscious

Combat Skills

Combat skills are special abilities purchased with XP that enhance your character's fighting capabilities.

Shield Bash
Warrior Combat Skill
Cost 2 XP
Prerequisites Shield Proficiency
Call "Shield Bash - Knockback!"

Strike your opponent's shield or weapon with your own shield. Target must take 3 steps backward.

Backstab
Rogue Combat Skill
Cost 3 XP
Prerequisites Stealth I
Call "Backstab [X]!"

When attacking from behind an unaware target, deal double your normal weapon damage.

Death & Dying

Unconscious (0 Hit Points)

When reduced to 0 hit points, your character falls unconscious:

  • Fall to the ground and remain still
  • You have a 5-minute "bleed-out" timer
  • You may be stabilized by a healer or first aid
  • If not stabilized within 5 minutes, you die

Death

When your character dies:

  • Lie still for at least 1 minute
  • Place a white headband or hold your weapon above your head to indicate you are "dead"
  • Proceed to the resurrection circle or check-in with staff
Resurrection

Most characters may be resurrected at the resurrection circle. This takes 10 minutes and costs 1 Resurrection Token. You begin with 3 Resurrection Tokens per event; additional tokens may be earned through gameplay.
