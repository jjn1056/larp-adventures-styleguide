Creating a Unit/Faction

Units and factions are player-created organizations that shape the political landscape of Osterra. From mercenary companies to noble houses, from religious orders to criminal guilds, these groups drive much of the game's ongoing narrative.

"The greatest battles in Osterra are not won with swords alone, but with alliances, resources, and the loyalty of those who follow your banner."

Formation Requirements

To officially form a unit, you must:

  1. Gather Members: Meet the minimum membership requirement for your unit type
  2. Submit Charter: File a charter with game staff including your unit name, type, purpose, and leadership structure
  3. Design Heraldry: Create a unique banner, colors, or symbol for your unit
  4. Establish Headquarters: Claim an in-game location as your base of operations
  5. Pay Formation Fee: 50 gold pieces (in-game currency) or equivalent resources
Faction vs. Unit

A Faction is an alliance of multiple units working together. Factions can claim larger territories and field larger armies, but require coordination between member units. Each unit retains its independence within the faction.

Land Ownership

Territory in Osterra represents not just physical space but also political power, economic resources, and strategic advantage. Controlling land is essential for any unit with ambitions beyond mercenary work.

Territory Types

Territory Type Size Primary Benefit Defense Requirement
Homestead Small Personal residence, small income None (protected by law)
Village Medium Population, basic resources Militia or guard presence
Town Large Trade hub, specialized craftsmen Garrison (10+ warriors)
Fortress Variable Military stronghold, defensible Standing army
Province Vast Multiple settlements, major resources Army + political stability

Claiming Territory

Territory can be acquired through several means:

  • Conquest: Defeat the current holders in battle and hold the territory for one full event
  • Purchase: Buy unclaimed land from the Crown or negotiate with current owners
  • Grant: Receive territory as reward from higher authority (Crown, Covenant, etc.)
  • Settlement: Develop unclaimed wilderness over multiple events
  • Inheritance: Receive territory through in-game succession or marriage

Maintaining Territory

Owning land comes with responsibilities:

  • Taxes: Pay quarterly taxes to your liege lord or the Crown
  • Defense: Maintain sufficient military presence to protect your holdings
  • Development: Invest resources to improve and maintain infrastructure
  • Justice: Resolve disputes among your subjects and maintain order
  • Fealty: Answer the call to arms when summoned by your liege
Losing Territory

Territory can be lost through conquest, abandonment (failing to attend events), rebellion, or legal seizure (failing to pay taxes or answer feudal obligations). Lost territory reverts to the Crown or the conquering force.

Resource Management

Resources are the lifeblood of any unit. They fund armies, build fortifications, craft equipment, and influence political outcomes. Managing resources effectively is key to unit success.

Resource Types

Gold

Universal currency. Used for purchases, wages, taxes, and bribes. Earned through trade, taxation, and questing.

Materials

Raw goods like lumber, stone, ore, and food. Required for construction, crafting, and army upkeep.

Influence

Political capital. Spent to sway votes, gain favors, or manipulate court proceedings.

Income Sources

Source Yield Requirements
Farmland Food + small gold Peasant population, arable land
Mine Ore + stone Mountains/hills, miners
Forest Lumber + game Woodland territory, woodsmen
Market Gold (trade tax) Town+ size, trade routes
Temple Influence + tithes Religious order, congregation
Academy Influence + skilled labor Large settlement, scholars

Expenditures

  • Army Upkeep: 5 gold per soldier per event (10 for elite troops)
  • Building Construction: Variable based on structure type
  • Territory Taxes: 10% of income to liege, 5% to Crown
  • Equipment: Weapons, armor, siege equipment
  • Bribes and Gifts: Political maneuvering costs

Unit Combat

When units clash in large-scale battles, individual combat rules are supplemented by unit warfare mechanics. These rules abstract larger engagements while still allowing personal heroics to influence outcomes.

Army Composition

Armies are composed of different troop types, each with strengths and weaknesses:

Infantry
The backbone of any army
Combat Value 1 per soldier
Cost 5 gold per event
Strengths Holding ground, sieges
Weaknesses Slow, vulnerable to cavalry
Cavalry
Mobile shock troops
Combat Value 3 per rider
Cost 15 gold per event
Strengths Charges, pursuit, flanking
Weaknesses Expensive, weak in rough terrain
Archers
Ranged support
Combat Value 2 per archer (ranged only)
Cost 8 gold per event
Strengths Pre-battle volleys, defensive positions
Weaknesses Poor in melee, needs protection

Battle Resolution

Large battles are resolved using a combination of:

  1. Strategic Layer: Commanders make tactical decisions before battle
  2. Combat Values: Total army strength compared
  3. Champion Duels: Player combats that modify battle outcome
  4. Modifiers: Terrain, fortifications, magic, morale
  5. Random Element: Dice or card draws for fortune of war
Player Participation

Players can directly participate in unit battles through champion duels, special missions (assassinate enemy commander, capture the banner), and rallying troops. These personal actions provide bonuses or penalties to the overall battle resolution.

Political Structures

Osterra has a complex political landscape with multiple layers of authority, from local lords to the High Crown. Understanding these structures is essential for navigating the game's political intrigue.

The Feudal Hierarchy

Rank Title Examples Typical Holdings Responsibilities
High Crown King/Queen, Emperor Entire realm Supreme authority, final arbiter
Great Lord Duke, Duchess, Marquis Province or region Regional governance, Crown vassal
Lord Baron, Count, Earl Town or territory Local governance, military levy
Knight Sir, Dame, Banneret Village or estate Military service, local protection
Commoner Freeman, Merchant Property only Taxes and service

The Council System

Major political decisions in Osterra are made through a council system:

  • The High Council: Great Lords meet quarterly to advise the Crown on realm matters
  • Regional Moots: Local lords gather to resolve disputes and coordinate defense
  • Guild Assemblies: Trade guilds meet to set standards and negotiate agreements
  • The Covenant: Religious orders convene to address spiritual matters

Political Actions

Units can take various political actions between and during events:

  • Petition: Request action from a higher authority
  • Treaty: Formalize agreements with other units
  • Accusation: Bring charges against another noble or unit
  • Betrothal: Arrange political marriages
  • Fealty: Swear or break allegiance to a liege
  • Claim: Assert right to territory or title
Intrigue and Espionage

Not all political actions are public. Units can employ spies, assassins, and agents to gather information, sabotage rivals, or eliminate threats. These covert actions are resolved through staff moderation and can have dramatic consequences if discovered.
