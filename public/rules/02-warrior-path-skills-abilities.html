The Warrior’s Path

Skills, spells, and abilities for Warriors

LARP Adventures

Tier 0 Abilities

Basic Weapon Proficiency: Players learn the basic combat safety rules, and the use of hand weapons, and throwables.

Armor Proficiency: Players learn about the armor that they wear and about its effective and safe use in play. A Warriors starting maximum Armor Point Total is 3.

Shield Control: Players learn the art of Shield Control and may use shields in combat.

Coup de Grâce: The Player learns how to finish a fallen enemy. A mortally wounded player can be engaged with to collaborate in a short scene that results in the full defeat of a target player.

Physical Representation: A 3-5 second engagement with a mortally wounded player for an epic style kill.

Tier 1 Abilities

Advanced Weapon Training: Study and become proficient in the use of Great weapons (Weapons over 5 and a half feet in length, or heavier than average LARP weapons), polearms, and dual wielding.

Archery: Players are taught the safety rules of archery at LARP Adventures and earn the use of bow and arrow in play.

Armor Repair, Non-metal: Repairs a single hit location of non-metal armors at one armor point for every 10 seconds.

Physical Representation: Repair Kit and RP of repair (15 Seconds)

Shield Repair: Repairs normal shield for use.

Physical Representation: Repair Kit and RP of repair (15 Seconds)

Warrior’s Will: A warrior, or group of warriors working in collaboration can interact with a magical construct, affect, or item to temporarily deactivate it and interact with the object, area, or item freely. The amount of warriors needed is equal to the tier of the casted effect.

Limitations: Each warrior participating must have Warrior’s Will. Each warrior counts as one Tier towards the deactivation of the magical construct or effect. When working in unison the magical construct will only be deactivated for 10 seconds per tier of the highest tier Warrior that participated. Some magical constructs put into play by GMs may be unaffected or impossible to bypass.

Physical Representation: 15 seconds RP for each tier of the magical construct/effect.

Create Minor Repair Kit: The warrior may now create a number of minor repair kits equal to half their tier rounded up per event. A minor repair kit can be used to instantly repair one hit location of AP 2 or lower armor or shields.

Physical Representation: A small box with LARP safe repair implements inside. Warrior should also have access to a reference sheet with creation date and number of uses.

Limitations: Repair Kits expire after 3 months of being in play

Prerequisite: Armor Repair, Non-metal

Tier 2 Abilities

Armor Training: The Player has gained experience and is learning how to best wear and fight in their armor. They can now add 1 to maximum AP. Typically done by layering armor, or upgrading material.

Prerequisite: Armor Proficiency

Metal Armor Repair: Repairs a single hit location of metal armors at one armor point every 10 seconds.

Physical Representation: Repair Kit and RP of repair (30 Seconds)

Prerequisite: Armor Repair, Non-metal

Weapon Repair: Repairs normal weapon for use, for each point of damage the weapon deals, 15 seconds are needed for repair.

Physical Representation: Repair Kit and rp of repair (30 Seconds)

Intimidating Presence: The Warrior, now self-assured and well trained, can strike fear into the heart of a single NPC of an equal or lower tier once per event. The fear effect causes its target to turn and run the opposite direction for 5 seconds, or to wither and not be able to engage in combat.

Limitations: NPC must be sentient to able to be threatened

Physical Representation: Direct eye contact from within striking distance, and an assertive dialogue that specifically mentions “Fear”

Cross-Training: At Tier 2 you may train in one of the other Skill Paths. Cross Training skills are repeatable. This skill allows the player to explore Tier 0 and Tier 1 skills in other trees at the cost of 2 skill points and require all necessary prerequisites of the specific skill.

Healer Training 1: The Player dedicates time to learning the craft of healers. They can choose one tier 1 or lower ability from the Healer skill path.

Mage Training 1: The Player studies the craft of the magi of the realm. They can choose one tier 1 or lower ability from the Mage skill path.

Rogue Training 1: The Player studies and watches the rogues around them. They can choose one tier 1 or lower ability from the Rogue skill path.

Tier 3 Abilities

Hardiness 1: Character has built up endurance to pain and abuse and is hard to stop. The player gets one additional hit per hit location.

Physical Representation: Brown Belt Favor with a single stroke

Limitations: Only effective for warriors wearing up to two Armor Points of physical armor per hit location, to a maximum of 3 Armor Points. Once a hit is sustained to a legal hit location, a minor heal can recover one hit point to that location. A Major Heal recovers two hit points. Upon death, no further healing or Roleplay is needed to recover Hardiness points. Effects that damage Hit Points or change them in anyway also alter Hardiness.

Sappers Toolkit: The player, already skilled in making repairs to armor and weapons, can now repair mundane structures and siege equipment. Every 15 seconds of Roleplay is one point restored to the structure.

Physical Representation: A hammer, shovel, or other “field repair” instrument.

Iron Will: The Player possesses unyielding bravery, and becomes immune to fear, and resistant to other mind altering effects. If a creature or player causes fear, or attempts to alter the mind of the warrior, the player with Iron Will can ignore the effect. Iron Will is not effective against the forces of corruption.

Limitations: Only effective against effects of an equal or lower tier to the Warrior

Create Major Repair Kit: The warrior may now create a number of major repair kits equal to half their tier rounded up per event. A major repair kit can be used to instantly repair one hit location of AP 3 or lower armor or weapons and shields.

Physical Representation: A small box with LARP safe repair implements inside. Marked with date of creation

Limitations: Repair Kits expire after 3 months of being in play

Prerequisite: Create Minor Repair Kit

Jury Rig: May repair any item with only the things at hand. May be used on armor, weapons, and any non magical items or mechanisms.

Physical Representation: Usage of an available item as a stand in for a logical repair prop as needed in a situation. This could be: spare rope to fix a ship, the back of a sturdy weapon to repair armor, a coin to assist in repairing a broken chest, etc.

Prerequisite: Metal Armor Repair, Shield Repair

Tier 4 Abilities

Renown: The player has gained notoriety for good or ill, a player with Renown should announce to the realm what it is they wish to be renowned for, so that their name is known far and wide. In negotiations a player with renown should be given special considerations, if a Renowned player is included in a land search, you may deduct 25% from the normal cost. (non-cumulative)

Mighty Blow: The character has developed the ability to gather and focus their strength into a powerful attack, a player with Mighty Blow does double damage with a single attack, a player using this ability with a two-handed strike, with any weapon type, also gains the ability to destroy/damage shields, structures, or chests with this attack.

Physical Representation: Role play and must include a declaration by the player that the strike is a Mighty Blow.

Limitations: May only be used once per combat at Tier 4, and one additional time per combat for every two tiers after 4th, up to a maximum of half the Players Tier per adventure day (rounded up).

Last Strike: Immense toughness allows the warrior a final blow after they have been mortally wounded in combat. A player with Last Strike may swing their weapon one final time at an opponent attempting to perform a finishing “Coup de Grâce” move before succumbing to their injuries and falling in battle.

Physical Representation: Role play and must include a declaration by the player that the strike is their Last Strike.

Limitations: May only be used once per combat. Can only be used when a “Coup De Gras” is attempted.

Imposing Warrior: The warrior’s ability is now well known, and can inflict fear on a single Player Character of a lower tier once per event. The fear effect causes a player to turn and run the opposite direction for 5 seconds or to wither and not be able to engage in combat.

Physical Representation: Direct eye contact from within striking distance and an assertive dialogue that specifically mentions “Fear”

Limitations: This skill may not be used on the same player twice in a row.

Prerequisite: Intimidating Presence

Cross-Training: At Tier 4 you may train in one of the other Skill Paths. The player must have the previous training level in the appropriate path to pursue the second stage of cross path training. Cross Training skills are repeatable.

Healer Training 2: The Player dedicates time to learning the craft of healers. They can choose one tier 2 or lower ability from the Healer skill path.

Mage Training 2: The Player studies the craft of the magi of the realm. They can choose one tier 2 or lower ability from the Mage skill path.

Rogue Training 2: The Player studies and watches the rogues around them. They can choose one tier 2 or lower ability from the Rogue skill path.

Tier 5 Abilities

Hardiness 2: Character has continued to build up endurance to pain and abuse and is even harder to stop. The Player gets two additional hits per hit location.

Physical Representation: Brown Belt Favor with two strokes

Limitations: Only effective for warriors wearing up to two Armor Points of physical armor per hit location, to a maximum of 4 Armor Points. Once a hit is sustained to a legal hit location, a minor heal can recover one hit point to that location. A Major Heal recovers two hit points. Upon death, no further healing or Roleplay is needed to recover Hardiness points. Affects that damage Hit Points or change them in anyway also alter Hardiness.

Prerequisite: Hardiness 1

Advanced Armor Training: Through extensive time in Combat, the warrior now gains an understanding in how to maximize the effectiveness of their armor, including the ability to layer armor extensively. This skill raises a character's maximum AP by 1. A Warrior now can combine armor layers to reach a maximum AP of 5.

Prerequisite: Armor Training

Rallying Cry: The player possesses the unrelenting bravery of Iron Will and is able to share their resistance to fear, and other mind altering effects. All allied players that can hear the Rallying Cry are also immune to fear, and resistant to mind altering based effects, any effects in play at the time the Rallying Cry is made are lifted or subside.

Prerequisite: Iron Will

Physical Representation: A field encompassing shout, battle cry, horn blast, or other implement to cause noise and garner attention of allies, that lasts a minimum of 5 seconds.

Brutal Rage: Through sheer will or years of training the warrior has trained their mind to briefly ignore its physical limitations. The player becomes rage incarnate and may take 5 hits from any weapon regardless of their current Armor Points or injuries after Brutal Rage is invoked. The Warrior may continue to fight in this way until all hits are expended or 60 seconds have passed, at which point they die, regardless of damage incurred.

Physical Representation: Role play, and must include a declaration by the player that they are entering their Brutal Rage.

Limitations: May only be used once per combat, up to a maximum of 3 times per adventure day.

Blood Oath: The character has been wronged in the past and holds enmity against a certain creature (typically a specific type of Monster) and swears a Blood Oath against them, usually one that marauds their homeland or done them some great injustice. Typical enemies include giants, orcs, lizard men, trolls, ect. The player gains double damage versus their declared mortal enemies.

Physical Representation: A scroll of the Blood Oath created by the player that declares their mortal enemy.

Limitations: Role play, a character that has declared a Blood Oath will actively seek out their enemy in combat in preference to all other foes, unless someone else presents a much greater danger. It is difficult for them to hide their hatred and it is only with great difficulty that they can encounter creatures that they have sworn a Blood Oath against without attacking them.

Sapper Constructs: The player already capable of impromptu repair is now trained in the art of combat engineering, having shown an aptitude to repair and build under combat conditions, the player amasses a kit of tools that allows them to half the time of repairs and gives them the ability to create simple offensive and defensive structures in the field. Structures appear as quickly as they can be brought to the field and may include the following:

Barricades: The player can construct up to 2 barricades + 1 for every Tier passed 6. Barricades may be up to 5’ feet wide and up to 2’ feet deep. They act as shields in reference to being destroyed and have 2 structural damage points at Tier 6, and +1 structural damage point for each Tier after.

Physical Representation: Barricades may be improvised with a pile of relatively safe brush or debris scavenged from surroundings, or prefabricated and put in place, alternatively the area may be marked with a rope, it must have a note attached that clearly marks it as a BARRICADE, and its structural points along with the makers mark and Tier.

Trench: The trench is a 5x10 foot area that players passing through must go on their knees.

Physical Representation: Represented with an outline of field paint, foam, or black fabric.

Watchpost: The Player can construct up to 2 at a time watch posts that a single player can stand on. The watchpost is a single “fenced in” 5x5 block raised from the ground no more than 3 feet with only a single “entrance point”, the location of which is denoted by a lack of fence on the 5x5 block. Players on the watchpost cannot be engaged in melee combat unless from this entrance point. If a player falls from the watchpost by going off a point that is not the entryway or if the watchpost is destroyed, they are rendered unconscious. Watchposts act as shields in reference to being destroyed.

Physical Representation: A maximum of a 5x5 area roped off as a structure, with at least one clearly marked entrance.

Battering Ram: The warrior may create a battering ram that can destroy normal doors and gates. A battering ram will do 1 structural point of damage per person operating it.

Physical Representation: Battering rams can be made in the field, but should be made LARP safe (no sharp protrusions, or splintered ends), and must be at least 2’ long per person using the battering ram.

Prerequisites: Jury Rig

Tier 6 Abilities

Elementalist’s Bane: The Warrior may now select a single element (Fire, Ice, Lightning, Necrotic) to specifically be resistant towards, taking only half damage from that source.

Prerequisite: Read/Write Arcana

Physical Representation: A sigil of the chosen element somewhere clearly denoted on the warrior.

Tactician: Once per event, the player can decide to re-roll a land search action, or any other encounter table roll. They may then choose which dice result they wish to use. This can be utilized for the NPC type, enemy NPC amount, or other NPC disposition.

Plant The Banner: Before a battle begins the warrior can place their standard as a rallying point for their fellow battlemates. Players within 30 ft of the banner are immune to fear or other mind altering effects as long as the banner stands. Additionally, mortalled players who are able to crawl back and touch the banner may return to combat with a single Hit Point, but limbs are not healed or armor fixed. Further healing can bring them back to full strength.

Physical Representation: Roleplay and placing a physical banner with a distinct player’s or faction’s emblem in the ground, or holding it. The spot where the banner is to be placed may be pre-set.

Prerequisite: Rallying Cry

Warrior’s Stance: A warrior may choose a specific weapon that they excel in, and a specific form they may take on the field of battle to deal an additional point of damage while firmly in that form. The form must be custom to the warrior, but can share similarities with traditional fighting stances or other fictional portrayals.

Physical Representation: A clear declaration of the stance they are taking, and a difference in form from their typical fighting style.

Limitations: The Warrior can only take the stance once per day, and may only wield the specific weapons and armor their stance allows.

Ironclad Shield: The warrior’s shield is made more resilient by careful reinforcement, and the experience of the warrior has taught them how to deflect devastating blows more effectively. Negates 1 instance of any shield breaking effect. . One additional shield breaking effect can be negated for every two tiers beyond 6.

Physical Representation: Silver ribbon tied to shield at minimum, but, modifications to the shield such as representations of metal bands, reinforcement, reskinning the shield, etc are highly encouraged

Limitation: Only works for the warrior with the skill. To restore break resistant effects, shield repairs must be performed

Prerequisite: Shield Repair

Cross-Training: At Tier 6 you may train in one of the other Skill Paths. The player must have the previous training level in the appropriate path to pursue the second stage of cross path training. Cross Training skills are repeatable.

Healer Training 3: The Player dedicates time to learning the craft of healers. They can choose one tier 3 or lower ability from the Healer skill path.

Mage Training 3: The Player studies the craft of the magi of the realm. They can choose one tier 3 or lower ability from the Mage skill path.

Rogue Training 3: The Player studies and watches the rogues around them. They can choose one tier 3 or lower ability from the Rogue skill path.

Tier 7 Abilities

Arcane Destruction: A warrior, with an imbued weapon of some type, can now deal damage to a magical construct, affect, or item. This may include wards, sanctified locations, and magical items. The hit points of the magical construct is equal to the casters tier. All arcane structures/items explode when destroyed, pushing players back 10 feet away from the structure/item and potentially destroying the weapon used or injuring those around the blast. (at organizer discretion).

Limitations: Weapon must be imbued with some effect

Prerequisites: Mighty Blow

Bloodlust: After completing a “Coup de Grâce” style finishing move, all wounds on the warrior are temporarily healed to full. Does not repair armor, restore hardiness, or reactivate floating hits. The effect ends after combat and the Warrior collapses and is considered Mortally Wounded.

Physical Representation: A finishing move that is about 2-3 seconds, along with a battle roar or other similar RP that lasts an additional 2-3 seconds.

Limitations: After each “Coup De Gras” style kill, the warrior approaches closer to a frenzied state. After an amount equal to half their tier (rounded down), they no longer can distinguish friend from foe until they are slain and are considered frenzied.

Prerequisite: Brutal Rage

Feared Field General: The Warrior’s prowess on the battlefield is now so known, that enemies often turn to flee before combat begins. Once per event, the Warrior may now inflict fear upon entire NPC groups to force them to turn and run the opposite direction for 5 seconds, or to wither and not be able to engage in combat.

Physical Representation: A mighty shout, horn blast, or other signifier, followed by a proclamation including the name of the specific target or NPC group, that contains the word “fear”, and the name of the Warrior invoking the ability

Limitations: NPCs must be sentient, Leader of NPCs must be of a lower tier.

Prerequisite: Intimidating Presence

Beast of the Battlefield: The Warrior may now take all damage in a hit pool fashion. All damage is dealt to a base of 6 Hit Points, with additional hit points added for their lowest AP legal hit location or maximum Hardiness Points, as well as typical means of adding floating hits (arcane armor, head/eye protection, etc.) The warrior no longer loses limbs during combat, and magic is dealt directly to their hit pool.

Physical Representation: A sigil of the chosen beast somewhere clearly denoted on the warrior.

Prerequisite: Hardiness 2 or Advanced Armor Training

Siege Commander: Any fortifications/constructions fielded by the warriors party, including those built with Sapper’s Toolkit, put into play by organizers, and/or siege engines, now have twice the amount of HP, and take half the amount of time to construct.

Physical Representation: The warriors seal/name placed onto the fortifications/construction

Prerequisite: Sapper’s Toolkit
