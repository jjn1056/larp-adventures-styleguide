Quick Start Guide

Everything you need for your first event in Osterra

Choose Your Path

Every character in Osterra follows one of four paths. Your path determines your starting abilities and how you grow over time. Don't worry about picking "wrong" — cross-training lets you learn from other paths as you advance.

Warrior

Frontline combatant. Heavy armor, shields, battlefield control, and fear effects. Start with 3 Armor Points.

Rogue

Stealth and subterfuge. Traps, locks, poisons, pickpocketing, and backstab. Start with 2 Armor Points.

Mage

Arcane caster. Combat magic, wards, enchantments, and necromancy. Spellbook-based casting system.

Healer

Support and divine magic. Healing, blessings, curses, undead control, and restoration. Start with 2 Armor Points.

Core Mechanics

Here's what you need to know before your first fight.

Combat States
  • Combat Ready — Full health, able to fight
  • Wounded — Limb damage, reduced capability
  • Mortally Wounded — Dying; can crawl and call for aid
  • Dead — Cannot engage until revived
  • Stable — Healed from mortal wound but not combat ready
Armor Points (AP)

Physical armor absorbs damage before your body takes hits. AP varies by path:

  • Warriors: Start at 3 AP, can reach 5 AP
  • Rogues/Healers: Max 2 AP
  • Mages: No starting AP (rely on wards)

Note: Metal armor interferes with Rogue stealth abilities.

Physical Representation (Phys-Rep)

All abilities require appropriate props, costumes, or roleplay. Spells need spellbooks, traps need cards, poisons use purple ribbons, and wards use yellow ribbon. We'll help you sort this out at your first event!

Your First Event

Here's what to bring and what to expect.

What to Bring
  • Costume or garb (we have loaner items available!)
  • Comfortable shoes for outdoor terrain
  • Weather-appropriate layers
  • Water bottle and personal medications
  • Sleeping bag/bedding if overnight event
  • A positive attitude and willingness to learn
What to Expect
  • Check-in and weapons safety inspection
  • New player orientation with a mentor
  • In-game introduction to the world
  • Combat training with experienced players
  • Quests and roleplaying encounters
  • Meals and downtime (events vary)

Safety First

Your safety and comfort are our top priority.

  • All weapons must pass safety inspection before play
  • No actual physical contact during Loot or Pickpocket actions
  • Bound players are never actually tied up
  • Consent is required for certain effects (e.g., being animated as undead)
  • Organizers adjudicate any uncertain situations
  • You can always call "Hold" to pause play for safety
  • Hydration and rest breaks are encouraged
  • Medical staff on-site at all events
