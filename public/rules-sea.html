Naval Combat Overview

The seas of Osterra are highways for trade, invasion, and adventure. From the merchant fleets of the Silver Coast to the dreaded pirate havens of the Shattered Isles, naval power shapes the fate of nations.

"The sea cares nothing for titles or gold. She respects only strength, cunning, and those who know her moods. Master the waves, and the world is yours."

- Admiral Thessa Stormwright, Founder of the Maritime Guild

How Naval Combat Works

Naval combat in LARP Adventures combines strategic ship-to-ship mechanics with live-action boarding actions:

  1. Encounter Phase: Ships spot each other and commanders decide to engage, flee, or negotiate
  2. Maneuvering Phase: Strategic decisions about positioning, wind advantage, and distance
  3. Ranged Phase: Catapults, ballistae, and magic volleys exchange between ships
  4. Boarding Phase: Crews engage in live-action combat if ships come alongside
  5. Resolution Phase: Capture, sinking, or escape determined
Representing Ships

At events, ships are represented by designated areas on the field, marked banners, or constructed set pieces. Movement between "ships" represents swimming, grappling lines, or gangways. Staff will brief players on the specific setup at each event featuring naval content.

Naval Skills

Characters can invest XP in naval-specific skills:

  • Seamanship: Basic sailing, navigation, and ship maintenance
  • Ship Combat: Operating siege weapons and understanding naval tactics
  • Navigation: Chart reading, weather prediction, and finding safe routes
  • Command: Leadership bonuses when captaining a vessel
  • Swimming: Survival and mobility in water

Ship Types

Osterra's fleets include vessels of all sizes, from humble fishing boats to mighty warships. Each ship type has distinct capabilities, costs, and crew requirements.

Skiff / Fishing Boat
Small Personal Vessel
Hull Points 10
Speed Fast (coastal only)
Crew 1-3
Cargo 5 units
Weapons None
Cost 100 gold

Perfect for short coastal trips, fishing, and smuggling. Cannot survive open ocean storms. No combat capability - flee or surrender.

Cog / Merchant Vessel
Medium Trade Ship
Hull Points 30
Speed Moderate
Crew 8-15
Cargo 50 units
Weapons 1 light ballista
Cost 500 gold

The workhorse of maritime trade. Reliable for ocean crossings with adequate crew. Light armament deters casual piracy but cannot fight warships.

Longship / Raider
Fast Attack Vessel
Hull Points 25
Speed Very Fast
Crew 20-40 (rowers)
Cargo 15 units
Weapons Boarding ramps, grapples
Cost 400 gold

Built for speed and boarding actions. Shallow draft allows beach landings and river navigation. Low cargo capacity limits range without coastal resupply.

Galleon / Warship
Heavy Combat Vessel
Hull Points 75
Speed Slow
Crew 50-100
Cargo 80 units
Weapons 4 ballistae, 2 catapults
Cost 2000 gold

The ultimate expression of naval power. Can engage multiple smaller vessels, transport armies, and project force anywhere with ocean access. Requires significant crew and resources to maintain.

Crew Requirements

A ship is only as good as its crew. Proper manning ensures safe voyages, effective combat, and profitable trade.

Crew Roles

Captain

Commands the vessel and makes tactical decisions. Requires Command skill. One per ship.

Benefit: Leadership bonuses apply to entire crew

Navigator

Plots course and reads stars/charts. Requires Navigation skill. One per ship.

Benefit: Avoid hazards, find optimal routes

Bosun

Maintains the ship and supervises repairs. Requires Crafting skill. One per ship.

Benefit: Repair hull damage during voyage

Ship's Surgeon

Treats injuries and illness. Requires Healing skill. One per ship.

Benefit: Heal crew during voyage

Master Gunner

Commands siege weapons. Requires Ship Combat skill. One per weapon.

Benefit: +2 to siege weapon accuracy

Sailors

General crew. Requires Seamanship skill (or training aboard). Variable number.

Benefit: Required to operate ship

Undermanning Penalties

Ships can sail with reduced crew, but suffer penalties:

Crew Level Effect
Full Crew (100%) Normal operation
Skeleton Crew (50-99%) -1 Speed, no repairs during voyage
Minimal Crew (25-49%) -2 Speed, cannot fight, no siege weapons
Critical (<25%) Emergency sailing only, risk of disaster each day
Hiring Crew

NPC crew can be hired at ports for 2 gold per sailor per voyage. They follow orders but won't take exceptional risks. Player crew provides better combat capability and loyalty.

Trade and Piracy

The seas offer both legitimate trade routes and opportunities for those with more... flexible morality. Both paths can lead to fortune or ruin.

Maritime Trade

Trade goods gain value when moved between different port types:

Goods Source Port Destination Profit Margin
Grain & Food Farming regions Cities, Armies 50-100%
Ore & Metals Mining regions Crafting centers 75-150%
Luxury Goods Exotic ports Noble courts 100-300%
Weapons & Armor Crafting centers War zones 100-200%
Magical Components Wizard towers Anywhere 150-400%

The Pirate's Life

Those who prey on shipping live outside the law but can accumulate great wealth:

Letters of Marque
Legitimized Piracy

A nation at war may issue Letters of Marque, authorizing ships to attack enemy merchants. Privateers operate legally (in their sponsor's eyes) and can sell captured goods openly in friendly ports.

Cost: 10% of all plunder paid to sponsoring nation

Capturing Ships

To capture rather than sink an enemy vessel:

  1. Reduce target to less than 25% Hull Points OR defeat all defenders
  2. Board the vessel and secure it (kill or capture remaining crew)
  3. Assign prize crew (minimum skeleton crew from your own ship)
  4. Sail captured ship to port for sale or personal use
The Black Spot

Pirates who become too notorious may receive the Black Spot - a bounty from the Maritime Guild. Bounty hunters and naval vessels will actively seek them, and most ports will refuse them entry. The only safe harbors become pirate havens like Freeport.

Sharing Plunder

Traditional pirate shares:

  • Captain: 2 shares
  • Officers (Navigator, Bosun, etc.): 1.5 shares
  • Sailors: 1 share
  • Ship's Fund: 1 share (repairs, supplies)

Port Rules

Ports are hubs of commerce, intrigue, and adventure. Each port has its own character, laws, and opportunities.

Port Activities

While in port, characters can engage in various activities:

  • Resupply: Purchase food, water, and equipment for the next voyage
  • Repairs: Fix hull damage and replace broken equipment
  • Recruitment: Hire new crew members or replace losses
  • Trade: Buy and sell cargo for profit
  • Gather Information: Learn about shipping routes, targets, or dangers
  • Shore Leave: Crew morale improves with time in port
  • Intrigue: Meet contacts, make deals, plot schemes

Sanctuary and Pursuit

Crown ports will not harbor known pirates. Pirate havens will not surrender criminals to authorities. Free ports observe neutrality - combat within port limits is forbidden, but what happens outside is not their concern. Naval bases only accept military or authorized vessels.
