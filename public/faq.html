Getting Started

LARP stands for Live Action Roleplaying. It's a form of interactive storytelling where participants physically act out their characters' actions. Think of it as improv theater meets a tabletop RPG - instead of describing what your character does, you actually do it!

At LARP Adventures, we use foam-padded weapons for safe combat, wear costumes appropriate to our fantasy setting, and work together to tell stories in the world of Osterra.

Absolutely not! We welcome complete beginners at every event. In fact, about 30% of our players started with no LARP experience at all.

Every event includes a new player orientation session where we teach you the rules, help you create a character, and pair you with experienced players who can show you the ropes.

  1. Browse our upcoming events and pick one that works for you
  2. Click "Register" and create an account in our Player Portal
  3. Fill out the registration form and select your ticket type
  4. Complete payment (credit card or PayPal accepted)
  5. You'll receive a confirmation email with all the details!

We recommend registering at least 2 weeks before an event so you have time to prepare.

Event costs vary by type:

  • Day events: $25-$35
  • Weekend campouts: $85-$135 (includes meals and lodging)
  • Special events: $25-$50

We also offer:

  • Early bird discounts (usually 15-20% off)
  • NPC pricing (reduced rate for playing monsters/NPCs)
  • Work-study options for those who can help with setup/cleanup

Events

Day Events run for a single day (usually Saturday, 10am-6pm). They're great for trying out LARP without committing a whole weekend.

Campout Events run Friday evening through Sunday afternoon. You'll sleep at the event site (bunks or tents), and the game runs continuously with some overnight elements. These offer the most immersive experience.

Special Events like Winter Council or Bardstock have unique formats - check individual event descriptions for details.

For all events:

  • Costume/garb (we have loaner items if you don't have any)
  • Water bottle
  • Any medications you need
  • Sunscreen and bug spray (outdoor events)

For campouts, also bring:

  • Sleeping bag and pillow
  • Toiletries
  • Flashlight or headlamp (red filter preferred)
  • Weather-appropriate clothing for day and night
  • Alarm clock or phone charger

Weekend events include all meals (Friday dinner through Sunday lunch). However, you're welcome to bring:

  • Personal snacks for between meals
  • Special dietary items if you have restrictions we can't accommodate
  • Beverages (alcohol is permitted for those 21+ but please drink responsibly)

Please let us know about dietary restrictions during registration and we'll do our best to accommodate you!

Gear & Costumes

We use a fantasy medieval setting. Good costume options include:

  • Simple tunics or tabards
  • Loose-fitting pants (avoid jeans if possible)
  • Cloaks or capes
  • Boots or dark-colored closed-toe shoes
  • Belts and pouches

Don't have anything? We have a loaner costume closet! Just let us know when you register and we'll set something aside for you.

Avoid: Modern logos, bright colors not found in nature, athletic wear, and anything that breaks immersion.

  • Waiting until after your first event so you know what style you like
  • Buying from reputable LARP weapon vendors (we have a list in the Player Portal)

Many of our players also make their own gear! Check our Discord for crafting tips and tutorials.

Gameplay

No! Our weapons are made of foam and designed to be safe. You'll definitely feel the hit, but it shouldn't hurt. Think of it as being lightly tapped.

We have strict rules against hard swings, head shots, and other unsafe combat practices. If someone is hitting too hard, just let a staff member know and we'll address it immediately.

Combat is just one part of the game! Many players focus on:

  • Roleplaying: Negotiating, politicking, and storytelling
  • Crafting: Making in-game items and running shops
  • Healing: Supporting fighters without fighting yourself
  • Mystery solving: Investigating plots and solving puzzles

We have a "non-combatant" ribbon you can wear to indicate you're not participating in combat. Other players will respect this.

Don't worry about memorizing everything before your first event! Here's our recommended approach:

  1. Read our Quick Start Guide (about 10 minutes)
  2. Attend the new player orientation at your first event
  3. Ask questions as you play - everyone was new once!
  4. Learn more detailed rules as they become relevant to your character

Experienced players and staff are always happy to answer questions during the game. Just ask "Hey, out of game question..." and they'll help you out.

Yes, but don't worry - death is rarely permanent!

If your character dies, you can usually be resurrected at the resurrection circle. This takes about 10 minutes and uses one of your Resurrection Tokens (you start with 3 per event).

Permanent death only happens if you run out of tokens or choose to retire your character. Even then, you can always create a new character and keep playing!
