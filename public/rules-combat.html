Overview

The C.O.M.B.a.T. System (Comprehensive Osterra Melee, Battle, and Tactical) provides the framework for all physical combat encounters in LARP Adventures. This system emphasizes safety, fairness, and dramatic storytelling while allowing players to engage in exciting martial confrontations.

"Combat in Osterra is not about winning at all costs - it's about creating memorable moments of heroism, tension, and adventure for everyone involved."

The C.O.M.B.a.T. System is built on five core principles:

  • Safety First: All combat must be conducted safely using approved equipment and techniques
  • Clear Communication: Damage calls and effect announcements must be audible and understood
  • Honor System: Players are responsible for honestly tracking their own damage and effects
  • Dramatic Play: Emphasize roleplay and dramatic reactions over mechanical efficiency
  • Inclusive Design: Combat roles and effectiveness are available to all player types
New to Combat?

Every event includes a combat training session during check-in. Experienced marshals will teach you proper technique, weapon handling, and the calls you need to know. Don't worry about memorizing everything before your first event!

Weapon Safety

All weapons used in LARP Adventures must meet strict safety standards. These requirements protect all participants and ensure fair, enjoyable combat for everyone.

Weapon Inspection

All weapons must pass safety inspection before each event. Weapons that fail inspection may not be used until repaired and re-inspected. The inspection process checks:

  • Foam padding integrity and minimum thickness
  • Core material safety and stability
  • Tip construction for thrusting weapons
  • Overall construction and durability
  • Appropriate weight and balance

Approved Weapon Types

Two-Handed Melee 72" 2 Must be wielded with both hands

Thrown Weapons 12" 1 Entirely foam, no rigid core

Prohibited Techniques

Never Do These
  • Head Shots: Never intentionally strike the head, neck, or throat
  • Groin Shots: Avoid targeting the groin area
  • Shield Punching: Do not use shields as offensive weapons
  • Charging: Do not run full speed into opponents
  • Grappling: No physical grabbing or wrestling without consent
  • Machine-Gunning: Allow at least 1 second between swings to the same location

Damage Calls

Every successful strike must be accompanied by a damage call. This verbal announcement tells your opponent how much damage they take and any special properties of the attack.

Basic Damage Format

The standard damage call format is: [Number] [Type]!

  • "One!" - Standard one-handed weapon damage
  • "Two!" - Two-handed weapon or enhanced damage
  • "Three Magic!" - Three points of magical damage
  • "Two Silver!" - Two points of silver damage

Damage Types

Type Call Effect
Normal "[X]!" Standard physical damage, blocked by armor
Magic "[X] Magic!" Bypasses non-magical armor
Silver "[X] Silver!" Effective against lycanthropes and certain undead
Fire "[X] Fire!" May ignite flammable materials, extra damage to cold creatures
Ice "[X] Ice!" May slow target, extra damage to fire creatures
Poison "[X] Poison!" Bypasses armor, may have lingering effects
Holy "[X] Holy!" Extra damage to undead and demons

Defensive Calls

When you negate or reduce incoming damage, announce it clearly:

  • "Armor!" - Damage absorbed by armor points
  • "Blocked!" - Attack stopped by shield
  • "Parry!" - Using a skill to negate the attack
  • "Resist!" - Immune to the damage type or effect
  • "No Effect!" - Attack has no effect on you

Hit Locations

Special Abilities

Beyond basic damage, characters can learn special combat abilities that create dramatic effects on the battlefield. These abilities require XP investment and often have limited uses per day.

Combat Maneuvers

Disarm
Warrior Combat Skill
Cost 3 XP
Prerequisites Weapon Proficiency
Call "Disarm!"
Uses 3/day

Strike the opponent's weapon. They must drop their weapon for 10 seconds and may not pick it up during that time.

Stun
Warrior Combat Skill
Cost 4 XP
Prerequisites Weapon Mastery
Call "Stun 5 seconds!"
Uses 2/day

A powerful blow that leaves the target dazed. They cannot attack, defend, or move for 5 seconds.

Vital Strike
Rogue Combat Skill
Cost 5 XP
Prerequisites Anatomy Knowledge
Call "Vital [X]!"
Uses 2/day

A precisely aimed strike at a vital point. Deal double your normal weapon damage.

Effect Durations

Blind 10 seconds Must close or cover eyes
Silence 30 seconds Cannot speak or cast spells

Death and Dying

When your character's hit points reach zero or below, they have fallen in combat. But death in Osterra is rarely the end - resurrection is possible, though it comes at a cost.

The Dying State

When reduced to 0 hit points:

  1. Fall to the ground (safely!) and remain still
  2. Begin your 5-minute "bleed-out" timer
  3. You may groan, call weakly for help, or roleplay dying
  4. You cannot move, attack, or use abilities
  5. You are aware of your surroundings but cannot act on that knowledge

Stabilization

A dying character can be stabilized to stop the bleed-out timer:

  • First Aid: 30 seconds of roleplay bandaging (requires First Aid skill)
  • Healing Magic: Any healing spell or ability stabilizes and restores HP
  • Healing Potions: Administered by another character
Stabilized but Wounded

A stabilized character is unconscious and helpless. They remain at 0 HP until healed and cannot wake up on their own. They may be moved, searched, or (unfortunately) finished off by enemies.

Death and the Killing Blow

A character dies when:

  • Their bleed-out timer expires without stabilization
  • A conscious enemy delivers a "Killing Blow"
  • Certain instant-death effects (rare, always announced clearly)

To deliver a Killing Blow, you must:

  1. Stand over the helpless target
  2. Place your weapon on their torso
  3. Count slowly: "Killing Blow 1, Killing Blow 2, Killing Blow 3"
  4. If uninterrupted, the target is dead

Resurrection

When your character dies:

  • Lie still for at least 1 minute of respectful death roleplay
  • Optionally, place a white headband or hold weapon overhead to signal "dead"
  • Proceed to the Resurrection Circle (location announced at event)
  • Spend 10 minutes in the circle, roleplaying your journey back from death
  • Spend 1 Resurrection Token (you begin each event with 3)
  • Return to play with full HP but no restored abilities or items
Permanent Death

If you have no Resurrection Tokens remaining, your character's death is permanent. You may create a new character and continue playing, or speak with staff about other options (some rare in-game effects can restore tokens or resurrect without them).
